## Overview

**Please Set a Password** is an audio-visual installation about mass surveillance and personal responsibility online. It asks the audience to sit with how easily private and semi-private spaces end up visible when camera software is left open to the network, and how normalised that exposure has become.

The title is deliberately plain: it reads like a forgotten default prompt, but it doubles as a direct appeal to anyone who installs or maintains these systems to actually secure them.

## How it works

The piece runs a custom process that **searches the public internet for hosts serving the web interfaces of unsecured surveillance cameras**, then **pulls and displays their feeds in real time** inside the installation. What appears on screen is not staged footage: it is live material from cameras that are reachable without authentication, which makes the viewing situation ethically charged by construction.

The **sound** is not a separate soundtrack: it is **generated in real time through audio synthesis shaped by image analysis** of the video stream, so timbre and density track what the image is doing. The aim is an immersive field where image and sound both push toward unease rather than spectacle for its own sake.

## Recognition

The work was awarded **first place in Electronic Arts** at the **Italian National Arts Award XV** (Premio Nazionale delle Arti Italiane), in **Sassari, Italy**.

## Skills involved

The project sits at the intersection of **networked systems**, **real-time media**, and **installation dramaturgy**: building a pipeline that can run continuously in a room, deciding what is acceptable to show and how fast the audience should be confronted, and keeping the technical frame legible enough that the political reading does not get lost in jargon.
